Cebu Pochero

If you want this meal outside cebu, you’ll have to be extremely precise that you want pochero served Cebuano manner. Most people know pochero as a beef stew with tomatoes. However, the pochero served here in Cebu is really a piece of beef shank cooked for hours until fork soft. It’s served in a tasty transparent broth with pechay, bamboo shoots and sweet corn on a cob, a lovely supper for those chilly days. It’s similar to Bulalo, but many argue that pochero has it’s own unique flavor. You may also have this meal (without the soup and veggies) served on top of a sizzling platter, smothered in gravy. It’s a fantastic joy to have every now and again, particularly the bone marrow component. Where to eat Pochero in Cebu: Marjo’s Pochero (a lot of us Cebuanos grew up eating here) at their new location at Gorordo Ave, Abuhan Uno near Robinson’s Fuente, Ekits Foodhouse in Mabolo by Sarrosa Hotel or Muvans in Century Plaza by Mango Square (ideal when you’re on a budget).

Steamed Fried Rice

It's like seeking for a needle in a haystack outside of Cebu. It's rice stir-fried in soy sauce, topped up with a beef, shrimp, onion, pea and spice sticky sauce. When eating dim sum in Cebu, it's a must-have. Henry Uytengsu of Ding Qua Qua Dimsum House invented it in the late 1960s. People prefer the original recipe above others who attempted to copy it. Eat-all-you-can Ding Qua Qua Dimsum House at JY Square, Harbor City in SM or Ayala, Dimsum Break branches, Bradexx on Katipunan St.

Chorizo De Cebu

We Cebuanos have a unique way of flavoring our chorizo (or as those from the north would call it, Longaniza). Our sweet and spicy chorizo is placed within a natural casing. The combination also contains more fat than previous recipes. Unlike Spanish chorizo, which is usually cured or smoked, we cook ours by grilling or pan-frying it. Chorizo is widely available at barbecue stands and restaurants. It is available at Larsian BBQ in Fuente Osmea, AA BBQ in A.S. Fortuna and Yakski Barbeque & Eatery in Capitol & IT Park.
